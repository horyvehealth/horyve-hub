A MESSAGE FROM STA-ËLLE:

The Support I Couldn't Find.

I entered perimenopause at 39 — far earlier than most women are told to expect. When the symptoms began, I had no idea what was happening to me. The brain fog. The nights drenched in sweat. The sudden anxiety. The tingling skin sensations. The back pain. The mood swings. It wasn't until I was 41 that I finally understood these symptoms had a name.

As I learned more, I discovered that many women experience perimenopause earlier than they expect — and that research suggests Black women may experience perimenopause earlier and often spend more years in the transition than White women. As a Black woman myself, that was something I had never been told. Looking back, I wish I had known sooner.

My friends and family were going through similar experiences too, and like me, they wished they had someone to guide them through it. No one had warned us that perimenopause could begin this early. We were all searching for answers and support that seemed impossible to find.

Then I realized women weren't the only ones searching for answers. Men were struggling too — with exhaustion, brain fog, changes in mood and motivation, and a growing sense that something wasn't right. Yet very few people were talking about it.

That's why I created He-Andropause — to provide the same clarity, understanding, and support that I wished had existed for me.

"You are not alone. You are not being dramatic. You are not falling apart. You are experiencing one of the most powerful biological transitions the human body can go through — and you deserve real support."

— The Founder · Horyvē · Montreal, Canada
